A piece of the Profit…Sharing

American’s new profit sharing program will pay out in March based on what we earned in 2016. Now that we’ve shared our earnings for 2016, we know what you’re thinking – how is this going to work and what will my payout be?

Let’s take a look.

Payout Steps

Step 1

Five percent of every pre-tax dollar we earn is put into a profit sharing pool to be shared by all team members – international or domestic, mainline or wholly owned – through level 4 who are eligible to participate in the plan. The first step is to take the total amount of American Airlines Group’s pre-tax earnings and multiply by five percent to establish the profit sharing pool. For 2016, it’s approximately $5.5 billion.

Learn More

Step 2

Next, we will calculate the payout percentage. To do that, divide the amount in the profit sharing pool (Step 1) by the total population’s eligible earnings. That results in the payout percentage for all employees, which for 2016, we estimate is approximately 3 percent.

Learn More

Step 3

Then, we will take the payout percentage and multiply it by each employee’s individual eligible earnings. This results in your individual award amount.

What are eligible earnings?

Most types of pay are considered eligible earnings, but there are special cases that aren’t included. This list isn’t all inclusive, but it does break down the most common types of pay.

The best way to estimate your eligible earnings is to look at your YTD earnings on your last paycheck of 2016. It’ll be higher because it could include some of the ineligible pay types, but for most people it’ll get you pretty close.

How many people are included in this profit sharing program? Which workgroups will be included?

The majority of mainline employees – more than 100,000 people – as well as employees of PSA, Piedmont and Envoy will be a part of this program. Employees at level 5 and above who participate in other at-risk compensation programs will not be included.

Are international employees included?

Yes. All international mainline employees are eligible for the profit sharing plan. Profit Sharing payments may be subject to local country rules. Any differences in your country will be communicated locally.

Will the profit sharing payout be the same dollar amount for everyone?

No. The payout will be a percentage of each eligible employee’s 2016 eligible earnings. So, the payout amounts will vary based on differences in eligible earnings.

Why did we report a pre-tax profit of $5.1 billion for 2016 if, for profit sharing purposes, we’re using a pre-tax profit of $5.5 billion?

The number we report excludes special charges and credits so that our financial results are more indicative of our ongoing performance and comparable to measures reported by other major airlines. For profit sharing, we exclude the cost of profit sharing and other at-risk compensation plans, which results in a higher number and increases the profit sharing pool for team members.
